--- layout: default #dnd id: #dnd type: 1 format: landscape poster_file: satyajit.png title: Dr. Satyajit Rath title_prefix: Rendezvous with #to render title written_1: "We all talk about 'immunity' to SARS-CoV-2, the virus causing the COVID-19 epidemic. We talk about 'not having immunity' and therefore having severe illness, we talk about 'increasing immunity', we talk about using vaccine/s to 'get immunity'. What does all this talk of 'immunity' against this virus mean? How does 'immunity' work? We will talk about how there is not one thing called 'immunity', but a set of very diverse physiological responses that sometimes function together as 'immunity' (and sometimes not!). We will also talk about what the field knows and what it does not know, both about immunity to viruses generally, and immunity to SARS-CoV-2 in particular.

Science in COVID times...
The interaction will be around the perception of ‘science’ during the pandemic. In these unusual times, should we be worried more, or less, about any lack we perceive of 'scientific temper' in society? Is there a specific societal responsibility on 'scientists' during such times (as opposed to 'normal' times)? Even if there is, can there be very different responses among 'scientists'? What could be the origin of such differences of perspective? How different is the relationship between science and society going to be in the post-covid world (there will be one!)?" #content written_2: "Dr. Satyajit Rath pursued his MBBS degree in 1979 and an MD in pathology in 1983. After this point, his career took a turn from medicine to research as he went on to do post-doctoral works at multiple institutes from 1983 to 1990. He spent almost three decades working as a scientist in the National Institute of Immunology (NII), New Delhi from 1991, until his retirement in 2017.
Currently, Dr. Satyajit is an adjunct faculty at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER), Pune, the Translational Health Science and Technology Institute (THSTI), Faridabad, and the Christian Medical College (CMC), Vellore. He is also a senior editor for the journal, eLife. His research interests include immune physiology, lymphocyte development, lymphocyte responses, macrophage activation and immunity in diseases.
